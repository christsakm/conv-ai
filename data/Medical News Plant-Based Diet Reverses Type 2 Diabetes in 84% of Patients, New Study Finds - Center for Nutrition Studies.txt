Medical News Plant-Based Diet Reverses Type 2 Diabetes in 84% of Patients, New Study Finds - Center for Nutrition Studies [<p>A new intervention called the NFI Protocol has been shown in a recent study to reverse type 2 diabetes in 84 percent of patients.</p>, <p>The results have been published in the peer-reviewed Diabetik Forum journal in Slovakia. The publication has also been hosted on the state-run National Diabetes Association website called SDIA, accessible to doctors. The public online link will be available later this month (but you can see a copy of the pending publication <a href="https://documentcloud.adobe.com/link/review?uri=urn%3Aaaid%3Ascds%3AUS%3Adfac234d-d226-48da-8e76-4fcc54e552e1" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">here</a>).</p>, <p>The groundbreaking results showed 32 of the 38 type-2 diabetic patients in the study came off medication after the 20-week protocol, which is a personalized whole-food plant-based diet plan.</p>, <p><img alt="BREAKING: Plant-Based Diet Reverses T2 Diabetes In 84% Of Patients, New Study Finds" src="https://cdn.nutritionstudies.org/wp-content/uploads/2020/02/release-1.jpg"/></p>, <p class="text-center"><small>The above table represents a summary of the results [in Slovak].</small></p>, <p>Founded by businessman David Hickman and biomedical scientist Zuzana Plevova, <a href="https://nfidiet.com/" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">the NFI Protocol</a> is specifically tailored to individuals based on height, weight, age, sex, medication, and associated diagnoses.</p>, <p>“I can’t find any literature anywhere which competes with this, let alone has the top professor in the country [supporting it] and no practising professors in the country that haven’t approved the results,” an NFI representative told <em><a href="https://www.plantbasednews.org/" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Plant Based News</a></em>.</p>, <p>The results have been authored by a number of leading diabetes academics, including doc. MUDr. Emil Martinka, PhD, head of the National Institute for Diabetes and Endocrinology in Slovakia; prof. MUDr. Ivan Tkáč, PhD, who leads all medical departments in the country as well as being the head of the University of Košice; prof. MUDr. Marián Mokáň, DrSc., FRCP Edin, who is in charge of all the diabetic doctors; and prof. MUDr. Peter Galajda, CSc – head of diabetes research, among others.</p>, <p>The 7,000-word publication finishes [via Google translate]: “An important part will also be to compare the results of the NFI diet and the usual dietary regimens recommended for type 2 diabetic patients, including PBDs (vegetarian, vegan).</p>, <p>“If the results of the study are favourable, dietary <a href="https://nfidiet.com/" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">NFI protocol</a> could become part of the dietary armamentary for the treatment of type 2 diabetes and patients with metabolic syndrome.”</p>, <p>The study represents the first of many publications on the effects of the NFI Protocol on type 2 diabetic patients. It is believed the results could be submitted to bigger journals later in the year, including Diabetes Care and The Lancet.</p>, <p>Slovakia’s leading media outlet PRAVDA recently published an article about the early results (a teaser for which can be seen <a href="https://spravy.pravda.sk/domace/clanok/540514-svetove-vysledky-slovenskej-lekarky-zlepsuje-zivot-diabetikov-jedlom/" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">here</a>) titled ‘Is This The End Of Type 2 Diabetes In Slovakia?’.</p>, <p><img alt="Pravda is Slovakia's biggest newspaper (Photo: PBN.
